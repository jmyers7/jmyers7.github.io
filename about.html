Theoretical mathematician by training, with a ph.d. in commutative ring theory and homological algebra.

In an insulated and siloed academic research environment that demands and rewards hyper-specialization, especially from young researchers trying to make a career for themselves, the writings and projects on this website represent my humble attempt to rebel. I want to learn how my training might be useful in other areas of science and human knowledge, and the posts on this website track my progress as I learn.

Some posts are purely expository, where I might offer my own take on a well-known piece of mathematics, while other posts explore applications. The first few series of posts focus on applications in machine learning, but I want to expand even further in future posts.
