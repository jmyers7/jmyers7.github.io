\prob Match the functions with the descriptions of their gradients.

\medskip
\begin{henumerate}{2}
\item $f(x,y,z) = x^2+y^2+z^2$
\item $f(x,y,z) = x^2+y^2$
\item $f(x,y,z) = \dfrac{1}{x^2+y^2+z^2}$
\item $f(x,y,z) = \dfrac{1}{x^2+y^2}$
\end{henumerate}

\bigskip
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] Points radially outward from the $z$-axis.\medskip
\item[(ii)] Points radially inward toward the $z$-axis.\medskip
\item[(iii)] Points radially outward from the origin.\medskip
\item[(iv)] Points radially inward toward the origin.
\end{enumerate}

\prob Let $f(x,y,z) = x^2+y^2+z^2$. At the point $(1,2,1)$, find the rate of change of $f$ in the direction perpendicular to the plane $x+2y+3z=8$ and moving away from the origin.
