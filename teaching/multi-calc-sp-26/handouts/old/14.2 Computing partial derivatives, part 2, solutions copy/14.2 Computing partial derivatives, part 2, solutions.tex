\prob The temperature at a point $(x,y)$ on a flat metal plate is given by
	\[T(x,y) = \frac{60}{1+x^2+y^2},
	\]
where $T$ is measured in $^\circ$C and $x$ and $y$ in meters. Find the rate of change of temperature with respect to distance at the point $(2,1)$ in the $x$-direction and the $y$-direction.

\bigskip
\textcolor{red}{We have
	\[T_x(x,y) = -\frac{120x}{(1+x^2+y^2)^2} \quad \tn{and} \quad T_y(x,y) = -\frac{120y}{(1+x^2+y^2)^2}.
	\]
The rate of change in the $x$-direction is then
	\[T_x(2,1) = -20/3 \approx - 6.66 \tn{ $^\circ$C per meter}
	\]
and in the $y$-direction
	\[T_y(2,1) = -10/3 \approx -3.33\tn{ $^\circ$C per meter}.
	\]}
