\prob Let $f(x,y,z) = x^2+y^2+z^2$. At the point $(1,2,1)$, find the rate of change of $f$ in the direction perpendicular to the plane $x+2y+3z=8$ and moving away from the origin.

\bigskip
\textcolor{red}{The normal vector to the plane (pointing away from the origin) is $\vec{v}= \ang{ 1,2,3}$. A unit vector pointing in this same direction is
	\[\vec{u} = \left\langle \frac{1}{\sqrt{14}}, \frac{2}{\sqrt{14}}, \frac{3}{\sqrt{14}}\right\rangle.
	\]
Then
	\[\nabla f(x,y,z) = \ang{ 2x,2y,2z} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \nabla f(1,2,1) = \ang{ 2,4,2}.
	\]
Hence the desired rate of change is
	\[f_{\vec{u}}(1,2,1) = \nabla f(1,2,1) \cdot \vec{u} = \frac{16}{\sqrt{14}}.
	\]}
